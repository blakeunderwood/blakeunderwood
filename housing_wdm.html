West Des Moines property value

(with Excel)

For this project we're going to research the West Des Moines housing market.

I'm going to use Stratified Random Sampling to collect data on houses, by seperating the West Des Moines area into three groups and gathering 4 data points from each group.

I'm doing this because the houses closer to downtown Des Moines tend to be valued less on average then the ones further into the suburbs and by grouping them I can get an accurate overall representation of the houses in all of West Des Moines.



how I divided up the three samples of the West Des Moines area.



After selecting the house samples im going to look up the houses building value and land value and plug the data into an excel spreadsheet.

video showing sample data collection.



what my data looks like after collection.



We can find the average land per square foot for house properties in West Des Moines using:

SUM(B2:B13) / 12

which is 15,856 sq. ft or about one third acre.



We can find the average house size per square foot in West Des Moines using:

SUM(C2:C13) / 12

which is 2,233 sq. ft.



Were going to calculate the percent change in land from 1993 to 2023 using the column for land value / sq. ft(1993,2003,2013,2023). first we will subtract the 1993 value from the 2003 one, then divide that difference by the 1993 value, and finally multiply the result by 100.

Formula for calculating percent change
(2.68 - 1.42) / 1.42 * 100 = 88.73%

Our first houses land appreciated by 88.7% from 1993 to 2003 not including inflation.

calculating inflation adjusted return
(1 + .887) / (1 + .257(inflation rate)) - 1 * 100 = 50.12%

Our first houses land appreciated by 50% in just a decade(1993-2003), after calulating the average of each house then plugging that into the formual I got the average change for land for each decade (accounting for inflation) is the following:

27.22% (1993-2003), -11.57% (2003-2013), and 27.93% (2013-2023),


Average housing per sq. ft in West Des Moines was 46.1 in 1993, 84.9 in 2003, 91.9 in 2013, and 146.8 in 2023. Doing the same calculation for the change in value and adjusting for inflation I get:

46.6% (1993-2003), -13.5% (2003-2013), and 24.4% (2013-2023),


Conclusion

Some houses increased in value by over 50% a decade well others stayed around inflation(24%,25%,28%) over the course of the three decades, in the end when looking at the houses that had value gain overtime versus the ones that didn't I would say the houses that appreciate greatly always had a reason for their increased value such as urban development buying up properties in the area, new developments around the house making the land more valuable, etc.
